# Small Low-Profile Sling and Satchel Options for a Leica M10 Travel Kit

## Executive summary

For your brief, the strongest overall matches are the **Wotancraft Pilot 3.5L**, **Moment Everything Sling 4L**, **Think Tank TurnStyle 5L**, and **Billingham Hadley Digital**. ## Fit model and evaluation method

Because your exact 28 mm and 50 mm were unspecified, I modeled the kit against **current Leica reference dimensions**: a **Leica M10 body** at approximately **139 × 38.5 × 80 mm**, a **current Summicron-M 28 f/2** at about **55 mm long / 58 mm diameter**, and a **current Summicron-M 50 f/2** at about **43.5 mm long / 53 mm diameter**. That yields a practical “body with lens attached” depth of roughly **93.5 mm** with the 28 attached and **82 mm** with the 50 attached. For bottles, I used the **Keith Ti3030 400 ml** at **147 × 70 mm** and the **Keith Ti3031 550 ml** at roughly **198–199 × 70 mm**. citeturn27view0turn28view0turn23search0turn22search0turn6search5turn6search14

I treated all fit calls below as **geometry-based estimates** from published dimensions, then adjusted them for the kind of bag involved. Soft flap satchels usually tolerate slight overfill better than rigidly padded zip slings; tapered slings also tend to have less useable depth at the edges than the headline dimensions imply. Those judgments are mine, while the underlying dimensions come from the cited sources. citeturn27view0turn23search0turn22search0turn6search5turn6search14

## Best options

### Wotancraft Pilot 3.5L

This is the best answer if you want the bag itself to feel as intentional and refined as the Leica kit. It sits right in your target size zone, officially supports **one body plus two lenses**, uses **water-repellent 500D nylon canvas** with a **YKK water-resistant zipper**, includes a divider, and stays very light at about **400 g**. Its styling also reads as **heritage travel bag first, camera bag second**, which is exactly the direction you described. The catch is simple: unlike the Think Tank, it has **no bottle pocket**, and unlike the Moment Everything, it does **not** really like the 550 ml Keith bottle inside. citeturn14view0turn14view1turn14view5turn34search3

### Moment Everything Sling 4L

This is the best travel-centric sling if your goal is a small, flexible bag that can disappear inside a larger work backpack and still carry a Leica kit. The dimensions are compact, the look is understated, the material is **water-resistant with PFAS-free waterproof coating**, and the bag includes a **removable divider** rather than locking you into a camera-bag layout. It is also the strongest answer to your bottle-inside question: **400 ml works well**, and **550 ml works dimensionally**, even though the latter is not how I would want to travel all day with camera gear. Its main weakness is exactly the one you already flagged by implication: **no true bottle pocket**. citeturn3view4turn5search6turn9search7turn34search1turn34search13

### Think Tank TurnStyle 5L

If the **real bottle pocket** is not just interesting but actually important, this is the most compelling compromise. Think Tank gives you a **tuck-away bottle pocket**, a **seam-sealed rain cover**, **customizable dividers**, breathable back-panel comfort, and an official fit claim of **one body plus two to three lenses**. For your Leica kit, it is the least risky recommendation from a pure fit-and-function standpoint. The tradeoff is that it is the most obvious **camera sling** in this top group and is a touch bigger than your ideal. Still, it is probably the most rational choice if you want to carry a camera and a small bottle without hacking the packing plan. citeturn33view0turn32view1turn32view2turn32view3

### Billingham Hadley Digital

This is the best small satchel if you want the bag to blend into business travel, hotel lobbies, meetings, and nicer restaurants without reading as technical gear. Billingham’s own sizing guide explicitly calls out a **Leica M10 with one lens attached and another to the side**, and the bag uses the company’s **3-layer waterproof fabric** with dense internal padding and a rain-shedding top flap. It is less “fast” than a zip sling and gives you no bottle pocket, but it is one of the cleanest, quietest Leica bags in this whole category. If your carry style can lean satchel rather than sling, it is a very strong match. citeturn18view0turn17search2turn34search2

The **Moment Slate 4L** is the best choice if all-day body comfort matters more than clean rectangular packing, especially for walking-heavy travel or casual outdoor use. The **ONA Bowery** is the best style-first alternative if you want a small waxed-canvas satchel that can still take camera gear, but it is heavier than the Billingham or Moment options and less bottle-friendly in practice. citeturn31view0turn35search0turn29view3turn20search1

## Moment 4L bottle analysis

The most concrete bottle question in your brief is whether a **400 ml or 550 ml Keith Titanium bottle** can fit **inside** a 4L Moment bag and be carried “like a lens.” For the **Moment Everything Sling 4L**, the answer is **yes for both bottles dimensionally**, with a big difference in how reasonable the layout remains afterward. The Everything’s quoted interior is **27.3 × 14.6 × 8.9 cm**. The Keith **400 ml** bottle is **14.7 × 7.0 cm**; the Keith **550 ml** bottle is roughly **19.8–19.9 × 7.0 cm**. Because the bottle diameter is **7.0 cm**, both bottles clear the bag’s **8.9 cm** internal depth and can ride **horizontally** across the bag’s long axis. citeturn9search7turn27view0turn23search0turn22search0turn6search5turn6search14

```text
Moment Everything 4L interior: 273 × 146 × 89 mm
Keith 400 bottle:             147 × 70 mm
Keith 550 bottle:             198–199 × 70 mm
Leica M10 + 28 attached:      ~139 × 80 × 93.5 mm
Leica M10 + 50 attached:      ~139 × 80 × 82 mm
```

That geometry leads to a practical conclusion. The **400 ml** bottle can reasonably replace a spare-lens bay inside the Everything 4L. If your camera carries the **50 attached** and the **28 is loose**, or if the body is capped and both lenses are loose, a 400 ml bottle is workable as a “lens-sized” object. The **550 ml** bottle also fits on paper, but it leaves only about **75 mm** of the long axis unused before padding and dividers are considered, so I would not count on a pleasant **camera + spare lens + 550 bottle** arrangement. In real use, the 550 turns the sling into something closer to **camera + bottle + small accessories**. citeturn9search7turn27view0turn23search0turn22search0turn6search5turn6search14

For the **Moment Slate 4L**, the story is less favorable inside the bag because the slate’s body-hugging shape, reduced effective cube-like volume, and pocket architecture make it less efficient as a bottle-inside camera organizer. Moment does give it **external lash hardware** that explicitly supports a **small water bottle**, which is a much more believable use case than carrying a 550 ml titanium bottle inside the main compartment. So if you want a 4L Moment and bottle-carry matters, the **Everything 4L is better for inside-bag bottle carry**, while the **Slate 4L is better for outside-bag bottle carry**. citeturn31view0turn35search6turn15search2
