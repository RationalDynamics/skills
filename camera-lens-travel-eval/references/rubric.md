# Rubric

Total: 100 points. Apply score caps after assigning preliminary points.

## 1. Problem framing and assumptions - 10 pts

- 3 pts: Recognizes the user wants light, high-quality travel gear for a Sony a7R VI/a7R V style body while liking shallow depth of field and 35mm as a default.
- 2 pts: Recognizes the comparison against Leica M10 with 28/2.8 Elmarit and 35/1.4 Summilux.
- 2 pts: Separates one-lens travel zoom, two-prime travel kit, and tiny/romantic carry kit as different use cases.
- 2 pts: Treats 35mm being possibly not wide enough as a central constraint.
- 1 pt: Notes that user preference and experience matter, not only specs.

## 2. Weight math: bag weight versus hand weight - 20 pts

- 5 pts: Correctly defines and uses bag weight as total carried kit weight.
- 5 pts: Correctly defines and uses hand/mounted weight as body plus currently mounted lens.
- 5 pts: Correctly compares Leica kit bag weight around 1173g to Sony a7R VI + 16-35/2.8 GM II around 1260g, about 87g heavier.
- 3 pts: Correctly compares mounted weights such as M10 + 28/2.8 around 835g, M10 + 35/1.4 around 998g, Sony + 35/1.8 around 993g, and Sony + 16-35 around 1260g.
- 2 pts: Notes that Leica still feels smaller/flatter even when scale weight is similar.

## 3. Correct recommendations and hierarchy - 25 pts

- 8 pts: Identifies Sony FE 16-35mm f/2.8 GM II as the strongest single-lens, light, full-frame, high-resolution travel zoom option.
- 8 pts: Identifies Sony FE 24mm f/1.4 GM plus Sony FE 35mm f/1.8 as the ideal two-prime kit for the user's taste: shallow DOF, 35mm default, wider travel option, high resolution, and reasonable weight.
- 3 pts: Handles the FE 40mm f/2.5 G correctly: excellent tiny Leica-like carry lens but too tight/slower for this user's core shallow-DOF and travel-wide priorities.
- 3 pts: Handles the FE 24/2.8 G correctly as an ultralight alternative, not the primary shallow-DOF answer.
- 3 pts: Gives a clear final recommendation rather than leaving all choices equally weighted.

## 4. Full-frame, APS-C crop, field-of-view, and sensor-resolution logic - 15 pts

- 5 pts: Explains that Sigma DC lenses are APS-C and not full-frame primary travel lenses for a7R VI.
- 3 pts: Converts Sigma 17-40/1.8 DC to roughly 25.5-60mm equivalent on Sony crop and Sigma 18-35/1.8 DC to roughly 27-52.5mm equivalent.
- 3 pts: Notes crop mode loses full-frame field of view and sensor area, reducing the a7R VI to APS-C crop output around 28MP.
- 2 pts: Explains that APS-C f/1.8 depth-of-field is closer to full-frame f/2.7 when matched for framing.
- 2 pts: Gives the 24mm crop-flexibility point: full-frame 66.8MP allows useful cropping from 24mm toward 35mm.

## 5. MTF/lpmm, resolution, and contrast as deciding factors - 15 pts

- 5 pts: Explicitly discusses MTF, lp/mm, or measured resolution/contrast rather than relying only on subjective reputation.
- 4 pts: Correctly characterizes the 16-35 GM II as not necessarily record-setting in center sharpness but very strong and even across the frame, with no major edge weak spots.
- 2 pts: Correctly characterizes the 40/2.5 G as high-center-resolution, very crisp, with edge/corner compromise wide open.
- 2 pts: Correctly characterizes the 35/1.8 as strong and useful but not perfect at full-frame edges wide open.
- 2 pts: Correctly treats Sigma 18-35/1.8 as optically excellent on APS-C but still the wrong tool for this full-frame travel decision.

## 6. a7R VI functionality advantage - 10 pts

- 2 pts: Mentions 66.8MP full-frame stills versus Leica M10 24MP.
- 2 pts: Mentions autofocus, especially practical travel/people AF advantages over manual rangefinder focus.
- 2 pts: Mentions IBIS/body stabilization; does not overstate lens OSS for lenses that lack OSS.
- 2 pts: Mentions video as a meaningful Sony advantage.
- 2 pts: Mentions EVF/live exposure/crop flexibility, or comparable modern-body convenience.

## 7. Communication quality and decision usefulness - 5 pts

- 2 pts: Presents concise tables or arithmetic clearly enough for the user to verify.
- 1 pt: Uses caveats without undermining the recommendation.
- 1 pt: Avoids overclaiming subjective qualities such as "Leica look" or "GM magic" as measurable facts.
- 1 pt: Is practical about actual travel use, not just spec-sheet optimization.

## Score caps and critical failures

Apply these caps after adding points:

- Max 65 if the answer recommends Sigma 17-40/1.8 DC or Sigma 18-35/1.8 DC as the main a7R VI solution without clearly explaining APS-C crop limitations.
- Max 70 if the answer fails to distinguish bag weight from mounted/in-hand weight.
- Max 75 if the answer says the Leica kit is dramatically lighter overall without doing or implying the actual weight math.
- Max 80 if the answer omits the Sony FE 16-35mm f/2.8 GM II entirely.
- Max 85 if the answer mentions the 16-35 GM II but does not identify it as the most viable light, high-resolution single-zoom option.
- Max 85 if the answer omits the 24mm plus 35mm two-prime option entirely.
- Max 80 if it makes no use of MTF/lpmm/measured resolution despite being asked to compare resolution and contrast.
- Max 80 if it omits the a7R VI capability advantage entirely.
- Max 70 if material weights are wrong by more than about 10 percent and the wrong numbers drive the conclusion.
- Max 60 if the final recommendation is primarily based on hallucinated specs or incompatible mounts.
