# Leica Water Sling Expected Observable Reasoning Traces

This eval does not require hidden chain-of-thought. It requires visible reasoning artifacts that let the user verify the conclusion.

A strong candidate answer should expose these reasoning steps in the final text:

1. Restate the decision problem: a small, stylish, high-quality, comfortable Leica M10 + 28 + 50 sling/pouch that can handle water somehow and fit in a business backpack.
2. State the key user discovery: 7L is too big for Leica-only carry; 3-4L is the sweet spot unless true bottle pocket or jacket overflow is required.
3. Preserve the user-tested rejections: WANDRD is out, Peak 6L is too large and not good for Leica/divider fit, Sony A7R + 24-70 should not dictate the Leica bag.
4. Compare Moment Everything Sling 4L, Moment Slate 4L, Wotancraft Pilot 3.5L, and Think Tank TurnStyle 5L V3.
5. Explain water strategy: internal 400ml/flat bottle is possible but leak-risky; external bungee/utility straps are workable; true bottle pocket means TurnStyle 5L V3 or larger.
6. Use the Keith bottle dimensions: 400ml is 70mm x 147mm and plausible/tight; 550ml is 70mm x 198mm and too tall vertically for most 4L slings.
7. Provide a packing layout: M10 + 28 mounted, 50 in padded wrap/pouch, small battery/card/cloth, water separated or external.
8. Distinguish style and comfort: Moment Everything is stealth/EDC, Slate is lightweight/active, Wotancraft is Leica-vibe, TurnStyle is practical/camera-sling.
9. Explain that these 3-5L options fit far better inside Aer TP4/CTB26/H2 than 6-7L slings.
10. Give a top-four list with conditional picks rather than a single overconfident answer.
11. Mention museum/crowd mode: Leica on strap plus minimal spare lens may beat any sling when bag restrictions or pickpocket risk matter.

When grading, reward answers that make these steps visible. Do not require exact order.
