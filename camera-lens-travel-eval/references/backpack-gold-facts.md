# Business Backpack Gold Facts And Expected Conclusions

This file defines the factual baseline for the business-travel backpack eval. The evaluator should grade candidate answers against these facts, allowing small rounding differences and noting that prices can change.

## User's normalized decision problem

The user wants a premium backpack for business travel and commuting, not a dedicated camera backpack. It must carry:

- 16-inch MacBook-class laptop.
- iPad.
- Cables/charger/admin items.
- Lunch.
- Light jacket or layer.
- Water bottle in a built-in pocket.
- Optional small camera bag/sling/capsule inside, primarily for Leica M10 + 28mm + 50mm and sometimes a larger Sony setup in a separate bag.
- Luggage pass-through for roller-bag travel.

Priorities are quality and functionality first, comfort next, style next. The user is 6'4", dislikes bags that look dumb/goofy/camera-y, and currently uses an Osprey Radial 26 that makes shirts ride up and looks awkward. The user cares about under-seat fit, but the realistic standard is “under-seat plausible when not overpacked,” not strict universal personal-item compliance.

## Hard requirements for the backpack eval

A high-scoring answer must treat these as hard requirements or near-hard requirements:

1. 16-inch laptop support.
2. External water-bottle pocket(s), not a bolt-on bottle carrier.
3. Luggage pass-through.
4. Enough main-bay volume for lunch + jacket + cables + a small camera sling/pouch.
5. Better under-seat geometry than the Osprey Radial 26.
6. Business-travel style: polished enough for airport/client/office use.

## Key known conclusions

A strong answer should converge on:

1. **Final top choice: Aer Travel Pack 4 28L, preferably X-Pac if the user likes X-Pac/weatherproof styling.** It best balances business-travel polish, luggage pass-through, water pockets, 16-inch laptop support, quality, and enough room for a camera module plus lunch/jacket.
2. **Best functional backpack-first alternative: EVERGOODS CTB26.** It has the right travel features, bottle pockets, luggage pass-through, 16-inch MacBook Pro support, and a slightly more backpack-native feel than the Aer, but it is less sleek/business-polished.
3. **Best sharper business-tech alternative: Black Ember Citadel H2 25L.** It has the style, structure, water pockets, luggage attachment strap, 16-inch laptop support, and premium construction, but it is more rigid/tighter and less open for a nested camera bag than the Aer TP4 or CTB26.
4. **Best lighter open travel-container alternative: Patagonia Black Hole Mini MLC 30L.** It has a large open main compartment and good travel practicality, but its laptop fit is less certain for a 16-inch MacBook because Patagonia says “most 15-inch laptops,” and its harness/business polish are less compelling than Aer/Black Ember/CTB26.
5. **CTB20 and Aer City Pack Pro 2 24L are good smaller alternatives, but probably too tight if the Peak/Moment/Wotancraft-style camera bag plus lunch plus jacket are normal carry.** They are better when the camera sling is occasional or the user prioritizes under-seat size above packing ease.
6. **The Osprey Radial 26 baseline loses for this use despite lightness and ventilation** because it is deep/framed/kickstand-shaped, lacks luggage pass-through, looks less business-polished, nests camera bags awkwardly, and the user already dislikes shirt ride-up.

## Under-seat logic

Use this as reasoning, not absolute law:

- Strict personal-item examples are roughly 18 x 14 x 8 in on American and 17 x 10 x 9 in on United.
- Aer TP4 28L, CTB26, Black Ember H2, and Patagonia Mini MLC are **under-seat plausible** if not overpacked, because their depth is about 7-8 inches, but their height is around 19-20 inches.
- CTB20 and Aer City Pack Pro 24L are closer to under-seat/personal-item geometry.
- The Osprey Radial 26 is worse because its 12-inch depth and frame/kickstand make it hard to slide under a seat and eat foot room.
- Penalize claims that any 25-30L travel pack is guaranteed personal-item compliant on all airlines.

## Camera-bag nesting logic

Candidate answers should explicitly reason about the camera bag inside the main backpack:

- A 3-4L Leica sling or pouch fits easily in Aer TP4, CTB26, H2, CPP2, and CTB20.
- 6-7L camera bags fit best in the Aer TP4 28L and reasonably in the CTB26.
- A 6-7L camera bag fits in the Black Ember H2 but takes a bigger share of its structured main bay.
- A 6-7L camera bag fits in the Patagonia Mini MLC very naturally as a travel packing object, but is not quick-access from the backpack.
- A 6-7L camera bag is tight in Aer City Pack Pro 2 24L or CTB20 when lunch and jacket are also present.
- For fast camera deployment, any nested sling is slower than a true side-access camera backpack. The correct workflow is storage/transport in backpack, then pull out the sling or carry the camera on strap when actively shooting.

## Expected top-four recommendation skeleton

A minimally strong answer should say something close to:

1. **Aer Travel Pack 4 X-Pac 28L** — best final choice: polished business-travel pack, good harness, 16-inch laptop, bottle pockets, luggage pass-through, enough open volume for lunch/jacket/camera sling; not strict under-seat when stuffed and a bit heavy/travel-baggy.
2. **EVERGOODS CTB26** — best functional alternative: water pockets, luggage pass-through, 16-inch laptop, excellent packing/camera-pod fit, better backpack DNA; less sleek/business than Aer.
3. **Black Ember Citadel H2 25L** — best sharp business-tech alternative: premium materials, security feel, bottle pockets, luggage strap, 16-inch laptop; more rigid/tight for nested camera bags.
4. **Patagonia Mini MLC 30L** — best lighter/value travel-container alternative: open packing and lighter weight; weaker 16-inch laptop confidence and less business/harness polish.

Mention CTB20/CPP2 as downsized options only if the camera sling is occasional or the user prioritizes under-seat fit over packing ease.
