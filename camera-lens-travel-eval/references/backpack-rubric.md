# Business Backpack Rubric

Score candidate answers out of 100 points using this rubric. Apply caps after adding points.

## 1. Problem framing and user constraints - 10 pts

- 2 pts: Frames the bag as business/travel/commute-first, not a dedicated camera backpack.
- 2 pts: Identifies the core load: 16-inch MacBook, iPad, cables, lunch, light jacket, and optional camera sling/capsule.
- 2 pts: Treats water-bottle pocket and luggage pass-through as hard requirements.
- 2 pts: Treats under-seat fit as important but not guaranteed for 25-30L packs.
- 2 pts: Uses the Osprey Radial baseline correctly: current bag is comfortable/ventilated but deep, awkward-looking, shirt-ride-up prone, and lacks pass-through.

## 2. Non-negotiable feature coverage - 20 pts

- 4 pts: Correctly evaluates 16-inch laptop support and flags Patagonia Mini MLC's “most 15-inch laptops” caveat.
- 4 pts: Correctly evaluates external water-bottle pockets and avoids recommending bolt-on holders as the main solution.
- 4 pts: Correctly evaluates luggage pass-through and does not rank no-pass-through bags ahead without caveats.
- 3 pts: Evaluates comfort/harness and weight for business travel, not just capacity.
- 3 pts: Evaluates style/business appropriateness, including “not dumb/goofy/camera-y.”
- 2 pts: Mentions cost/value in a relative way without over-weighting price above quality/functionality.

## 3. Size, weight, under-seat, and camera-nesting reasoning - 20 pts

- 4 pts: Gives materially correct dimensions/weights for Aer TP4 28L, CTB26, Black Ember H2, and Patagonia Mini MLC.
- 4 pts: Explains under-seat geometry: 7-8 inch depth is good; 19-20 inch height is plausible but not strict; Osprey's 12 inch depth is bad.
- 4 pts: Explains how a 3-4L camera pouch/sling fits differently from a 6-7L camera sling.
- 3 pts: Correctly states that Aer TP4 and CTB26 fit a nested camera sling more comfortably than 24L/20L alternatives.
- 3 pts: Correctly states that H2 is stylish and structured but less open/tolerant than Aer TP4 or CTB26.
- 2 pts: Notes that nested sling access is slower than side-access camera backpacks; active shooting means camera out or sling removed.

## 4. Recommendation hierarchy and final output - 25 pts

- 8 pts: Ranks **Aer Travel Pack 4 28L** as the best final choice or, if not ranked first, gives a very strong and user-specific reason while still identifying it as the target to beat.
- 5 pts: Ranks **EVERGOODS CTB26** as a top functional/backpack-first alternative with correct tradeoffs.
- 4 pts: Ranks **Black Ember Citadel H2** as a top polished business-tech alternative with correct rigidity/volume caveat.
- 4 pts: Ranks **Patagonia Mini MLC 30L** as a top open-travel/value alternative with laptop/harness/style caveats.
- 2 pts: Mentions **Aer City Pack Pro 2 24L** and/or **CTB20** as smaller alternatives rather than main picks for the full load.
- 2 pts: Gives a clear top-four list with reasons, not a flat list of options.

## 5. Quality, comfort, style, and real-world use - 15 pts

- 3 pts: Evaluates materials/build quality and weather resistance without treating every premium bag as equal.
- 3 pts: Evaluates harness comfort and bag weight for all-day travel with laptop/camera/water.
- 3 pts: Addresses shirt ride-up risk or bag stability/close-to-back carry based on the user's Osprey complaint.
- 3 pts: Evaluates business-travel aesthetics: Aer/Black Ember more polished, CTB more utilitarian, Patagonia more casual.
- 3 pts: Describes a practical packing architecture for the chosen bag.

## 6. Communication quality and decision usefulness - 10 pts

- 3 pts: Uses concise comparison tables or explicit tradeoff bullets.
- 2 pts: States caveats clearly without undermining the recommendation.
- 2 pts: Distinguishes “under-seat plausible” from “guaranteed personal item.”
- 2 pts: Avoids overfitting to camera-bag logic when the main bag is business-first.
- 1 pt: Gives a practical test plan or purchase/return/loadout suggestion.

## Score caps and critical failures

Apply the strictest applicable cap after adding points:

- Max 65 if the top recommendation lacks either external water-bottle pockets or luggage pass-through and the answer does not acknowledge the violation.
- Max 70 if the answer recommends CTB20, CHZ, CPL, GR1 Slick 21L, or another smaller/no-pass-through/no-bottle bag as the main answer while ignoring the user's normal full load and hard requirements.
- Max 75 if it omits under-seat sizing/geometry entirely.
- Max 75 if it omits Aer Travel Pack 4 28L entirely.
- Max 82 if it discusses Aer TP4 but fails to identify it as the final/top choice or target-to-beat for this user's settled requirements.
- Max 80 if it ignores camera-bag nesting and only compares laptop backpacks abstractly.
- Max 70 if it confidently claims Aer TP4, CTB26, H2, or Mini MLC are guaranteed strict personal-item bags on all airlines.
- Max 70 if major product specs are wrong by more than about 10 percent and the wrong numbers drive the conclusion.
- Max 60 if the final recommendation is primarily based on hallucinated products, incompatible laptop sizes, or generic Amazon/unknown bags despite the premium-quality brief.
