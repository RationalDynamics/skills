# Business Backpack Expected Observable Reasoning Traces

This eval does not require hidden chain-of-thought. It requires visible reasoning artifacts that let the user verify the conclusion.

A strong candidate answer should expose these reasoning steps in the final text:

1. Restate the decision problem: premium business-travel backpack for a 16-inch MacBook, iPad, cables, lunch, light jacket, water bottle, luggage pass-through, and optional camera sling/capsule.
2. State that the bag is business/travel-first, not a dedicated camera backpack.
3. Treat external bottle pockets and luggage pass-through as hard requirements because the user explicitly disliked separate bottle holders and said pass-through is important.
4. Compare Aer Travel Pack 4 28L, Black Ember Citadel H2, Patagonia Mini MLC, EVERGOODS CTB26, and CTB20 or Aer City Pack Pro 24L.
5. Use actual dimensions/weights to explain under-seat fit: depth matters; 7-8 inch depth is plausible; 19-20 inch height is not strict personal-item compliance.
6. Compare against the Osprey Radial 26 baseline: lighter/cooler but too deep/framed, no luggage pass-through, awkward camera-bag nesting, not stylish enough, and already causes shirt ride-up.
7. Explain camera-bag nesting: 3-4L Leica slings fit easily; 6-7L bags fit best in Aer TP4 or CTB26; H2 fits but is more rigid/tight; Mini MLC packs them well but not for quick access.
8. Explain daily-use tradeoffs: CTB20/City Pack Pro are cleaner and more under-seat-friendly but tight with lunch + jacket + camera sling; Aer TP4 is roomier but more travel-bag-like.
9. Rank Aer TP4 28L first, unless giving a very explicit reason to favor CTB26 while still saying Aer is the user's settled final target.
10. Give a top-four recommendation with short labels and caveats: Aer TP4, CTB26, Black Ember H2, Patagonia Mini MLC.
11. Include a practical packing plan: laptop/iPad in laptop area, camera sling in main bay, lunch/jacket around it, water in exterior pocket, cables in admin/tech pockets.
12. State a test plan: load actual laptop/iPad/camera sling/lunch/jacket/water at home and test under-seat-style packing and shirt ride-up.

When grading, reward answers that make these steps visible. Do not require exact order.
